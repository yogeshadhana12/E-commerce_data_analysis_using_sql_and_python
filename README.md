# E-commerce Data Analysis (SQL + Python)

## Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing an e-commerce dataset to extract meaningful business insights related to customer behavior, sales trends, and revenue performance. The analysis was performed using SQL for data querying and Python for data visualization in Jupyter Notebook.

A total of 15 business problems were solved to understand patterns in customer purchases, product category performance, and revenue growth.

The objective of this project is to demonstrate how SQL and Python can be used together to transform raw transactional data into actionable business insights.

---

## Tools & Technologies Used

- SQL
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

---

## Dataset Tables

The dataset contains multiple relational tables including:

- customers
- orders
- order_items
- products
- payments
- sellers
- geolocation

These tables were joined to analyze customer behavior, product sales, and revenue metrics.

---

## Key Business Problems Solved

1. Identify unique customer cities
2. Count orders placed in a specific year
3. Calculate sales per product category
4. Analyze installment payment behavior
5. Identify customer distribution by state
6. Calculate monthly order trends
7. Average number of products per order by city
8. Revenue contribution by product category
9. Correlation between product price and purchase frequency
10. Moving average of customer spending
11. Cumulative monthly revenue
12. Year-over-year revenue growth
13. Customer retention rate
14. Top customers based on total spending
15. Sales trends and revenue insights

---

## Key Insights

- Certain product categories contribute a large percentage of total revenue.
- Order volume shows seasonal trends across different months.
- Customer spending patterns vary across cities and states.
- Revenue growth increased significantly during the early years of the dataset.
- A small number of customers contribute a large portion of total revenue.
